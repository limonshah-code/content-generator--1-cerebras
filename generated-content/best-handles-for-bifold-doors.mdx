## FAQ

**What is the best handles for bifold doors for beginners?**  
Start with a basic zinc diecast knob that has a wide base and a comfortable diameter. Look for a product that includes all necessary screws so you can install it without extra purchases.

**How much should I spend on handles for bifold doors?**  
Expect to spend between $10 and $40 per knob depending on finish and brand. Multi‑packs can lower the cost per piece, while premium finishes may rise toward $70 for a set.

**What features are most important in handles for bifold doors?**  
Key features include a wide base to protect hollow panels, a large enough diameter for easy gripping, a durable material such as zinc diecast, and a finish that matches your décor. Include the right screw lengths for your door thickness.

**How do I maintain my handles for bifold doors?**  
Wipe the knobs with a soft damp cloth and mild soap as needed. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can dull the finish. For metal finishes, apply a light oil coating periodically to prevent corrosion.

**Are expensive handles for bifold doors worth it?**  
Higher‑priced knobs often use premium finishes like brushed nickel or antique brass and have a heavier feel that signals quality. If you value long‑term appearance and a luxury feel, the extra cost can be justified.

**What brands make the best handles for bifold doors?**  
Prime‑Line, trust4care, Goldenwarm and DonYoung consistently receive high marks for durability, finish quality and customer support.

**How long do handles for bifold doors typically last?**  
With proper installation and routine cleaning, a well‑made zinc diecast or brass knob can last 5‑10 years or more before the finish shows noticeable wear.

**Can I find good handles for bifold doors on a budget?**  
Yes, multi‑packs of matte black zinc alloy knobs or basic satin nickel options provide solid performance at lower price points while still offering the essential wide base and grip.

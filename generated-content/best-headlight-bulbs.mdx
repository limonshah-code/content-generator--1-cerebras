## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Headlight Bulbs

### Find Your Need
Consider whether you need replacements for low beam, high beam, fog lights or a combination. Different beam patterns and intensities serve distinct driving scenarios, so match the bulb type to your primary use case.

### Budget
Set realistic price expectations. Budget halogen options typically range from $10‑$30 per pair, while premium LED or HID models can reach $80‑$150. Knowing your budget helps narrow choices quickly.

### Key Features
Look for lumen output, color temperature, beam pattern and whether the bulb is CAN‑bus compatible. Higher lumens and a cool white (6500K) color lend better night visibility, while a proper beam pattern avoids glare.

### Quality & Durability
Materials such as aviation‑grade aluminium, copper heat sinks and sturdy filament designs indicate longevity. Bulbs rated for 50,000+ hours or featuring IP68 sealing will outlast cheap alternatives.

### Brand Reputation
Established manufacturers like SYLVANIA, Termitor and AUXITO have proven track records and extensive vehicle fit databases, reducing the risk of incompatibility.

### Portability/Size
Check the physical dimensions (1:1 mini design) to ensure a hassle‑free swap. Smaller form factors simplify installation and avoid the need for extra adapters.

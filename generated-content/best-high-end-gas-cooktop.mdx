A professional chef may need a high‑output wok burner, while a small‑space apartment dweller might prioritize a compact layout. Consider whether you need a built‑in drop‑in model, a freestanding unit, or a portable stovetop for an RV or outdoor kitchen. Also verify that the fuel type matches your home’s supply—natural gas, propane or both.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
High end gas cooktops can span a wide price spectrum. Expect flagship models with premium materials and multiple burners to sit between $1,200 and $2,500, while compact but quality units may fall in the $600 to $1,000 range. Factor in additional costs such as installation, cutout modifications and any necessary conversion kits.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for burner output (BTU), thermal efficiency rating, flame‑failure detection, ignition type (pulse or manual), and material quality (304 stainless steel, cast iron grates). A reversible grill‑griddle, multiple burner sizes and independent controls can greatly expand cooking flexibility.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Stainless‑steel panels resist corrosion and discoloration. Cast‑iron or heavy‑gauge grates support large pots without bending. Sealed burners prevent food from falling inside, simplifying cleaning. Certifications such as CSA or ETL indicate compliance with safety standards.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands with a track record of kitchen appliances often provide better warranty support and spare parts availability. Research user reviews, warranty length (typically 1‑2 years) and the company’s customer service responsiveness before committing.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure the cutout dimensions of your countertop or RV cabinet. Remember that larger units may require reinforced support and proper ventilation. For mobile setups, a dual‑fuel system and manual ignition can be advantageous.
